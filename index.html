What is this?

In The Black Swan, Nassim Taleb illustrates income inequality by describing two societies, Mediocristan and Extremistan.

Mediocristan is a society where outliers don't significantly skew the total.
Extremistan is a society where outliers significantly skew the total.

In Taleb's example, citizens' weights are determined by these value distributions. So, in Mediocristan, the heaviest person is no more than a couple times the weight of the average person. In Extremistan, the difference is much larger [1].

For this visualization I've represented Mediocristan as Denmark, and Extremistan as South Africa. Denmark has a relatively low Gini coefficient (G=0.24), and South Africa a relatively high one (G=0.631). Gini coefficients are a common measure of inequality [2].

Each circle represents a citizen, and their number is their weight. A person's weight is a proxy for their relative wealth. Toggle and see the difference.

[1] For more info, see: Mediocristan & Extremistan.
